Hi! I am a postdoctoral researcher from the Dernburg Lab at University of California, Berkeley. I am interested in various chromosome biology relevant topics including (not exhausting) a bunch of when / what / how / whys related to chromosome organization and behaviors during cell division, cell cycle progression and genome evolution.

Experience

I'm now having fun with Caenorhabditis elegans.

Postdoctoral (2015 - present)

w/ Dr Abby Dernburg @ Univeristy of California, Berkeley

After my graduate training, I joined Dr. Abby Dernburg's group at UC Berkeley. The Dernburg Lab is doing a bunch of really cool things to study meiosis. With Abby, I am looking at the interplay between DNA Damage Response (DDR) and meiotic chromosome axis assembly from a mechanistic point of view. The model organism I use is Caenorhabditis elegans, which is an extremely powerful and versatile tool that potentiate my research.

Postgrad (2006 - 2007)

w/ Dr Baohua Zhao @ College of Life Science, Hebei Normal University

I've been able to have my first taste about what a real biology experiment looks like (not surprisingly very different from those on textbooks) in Dr. Baohua Zhao's Lab. The main goal of the lab is to seek strategies for the prevention of wide-spreaded zoonotic diseases. I got experiences in molecular cloning, and have been working with several types of microbes. It's also very valuable that I've learned some basic skills in working with mice.

Undergrad and even before (somewhere - 2006)

I became into Biology really late, and in a relatively unusual way - reading college textbooks when I was an undergraduate student. I had no idea about what biology would be like, even in the first two years of my college life. But, hey! Every biologist must have his/her own story, which does not have to be the same.
