Postdoctoral Research

My current research mainly focuses on dissecting the phosphoregulatory network that guides and shapes meiotic progression, using C. elegans as the model system.

Working with Dr. Abby Dernburg, I employ genetic, cytological, together with genome-scale quantitative methods simultaneously in my study. With my efforts, I aim to gain a better understanding of how meiosis is performed and regulated, in that beautiful way.

Graduate Research (II)

During my graduate training at IBP, my work has been focusing on a specified chromosomal region called centromere. Centromere is the place where kinetochore assembles during each mitotic cell cycle. It must be tightly regulated in order to accurately segregate the chromosomes.

Mentored by Dr. Guohong Li, I've been studying this topic through looking at a reversible phosphorylation event that takes place on the centromere determinant called CENP-A. This project was partially inspired by the crystal structure of CENP-A with its chaperone, HJURP. The latter work is led by Dr. Rui-Ming Xu's Group.

Graduate Research (I)

I've been studying a family of enzymes called phenylacetic acid-CoA ligases. By doing simple yet straightforward enzymetic kinetics, I learned fundamental experiment skills from Dr. Fu-Qiang Wang. I've also been working in Dr. Baohua Zhao's Lab for a year where I learned some knowledge of Immunity.
